Chapati and Ndengu Stew Recipe

A delicacy best served with green sukumawiki,avocado!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 cup of red lentils (Ndengu)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. For Chapati: In a bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead until you have a smooth, elastic dough. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them out into thin circles. Heat a pan over medium-high heat and cook the chapatis, flipping them until they puff up and are lightly browned.
  2. For Ndengu Stew: Rinse the red lentils (Ndengu) and cook them in a pot of water until tender, then drain and set aside.
  3. In a separate pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and garlic. Saut√© until the onions are translucent.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes, bell pepper, and carrot to the pot. Cook for a few minutes until the vegetables start to soften.
  5. Stir in the ground cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper, then add the cooked lentils. Add enough water to achieve your desired stew consistency.
  6. Simmer the Ndengu stew for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  7. Serve the Ndengu stew with freshly cooked Chapati on the side.
